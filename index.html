The problem

Merchandisers spend their days fighting fires instead of improving the business.

Every day, merchandising teams gather context across reports, spreadsheets, emails, and planning systems. Manual work keeps them reacting to immediate problems instead of making more strategic commercial decisions. Each function works from its own metrics and priorities, so conflicting decisions may never become visible. And because the reasoning, action, and outcome are rarely captured together, the organization starts from scratch each time.

1

Detect / ingest

An existing report or a bounded deterministic check creates the record and preserves when the issue became visible. Separate monitors surface the leakage after visibility: nobody accepts ownership, an approval goes overdue, an approved action never starts, or the outcome is never measured.

2

Reconcile

The context that lives in six places lands on one card: stock cover, sell-through against plan, open orders, supplier lead times - and the vendor email that quietly moved the truck. Every signal cited to its source row or document. Nothing asserted without a receipt.

3

Scenario

Options prepared before anyone asks, from your own playbook: open a transfer scenario, request a buyer review, prepare a markdown review, accept the risk and monitor. Each option carries its arithmetic - never a black-box score.

4

Route

Decisions get owners, not threads. The buyer approves reorders; allocation runs the transfer; store ops confirms the floor. Routed with the full context attached, tracked until the action actually happens - in your systems, with a named approval.

5

Measure

Did it pay? Recovered sales, availability, markdown avoided - measured on your numbers and written back to the record. Delayed and missed actions surface instead of vanishing. The graded history of every call becomes an asset your team owns.

Honest AI - our favorite sales pitch

No black-box forecasts. No autonomous agents inside your systems. The math is published.

AI models are not reliable reasoners - they are excellent accelerators and researchers. So no model ever computes your money. It appears exactly three times: reading and reconciling messy sources, proposing scenarios and rules a human adopts or edits, and narrating results after the numbers are final.

FAQ

Questions retail teams ask before getting started.

What does Outturn actually do?

Outturn continuously reviews the signals across your reports, emails, sales, inventory, and planning systems. It identifies the commercial decisions that need attention, prepares the relevant context, and brings them to the right person in priority order.

Is Outturn autonomous?

Outturn prepares decisions, but people remain in control. It brings together the context, options, and commercial impact, then routes the decision to the right owner. Actions are only triggered after approval.

Does Outturn replace our existing systems?

No. Outturn works across your existing planning, allocation, ERP, BI, and communication systems. It connects the signals between them and creates a shared decision layer above them.

Is Outturn a forecasting tool?

No. Outturn can use forecasts as one source of context, but it does not replace your forecasting tools. It helps teams decide what to do when sales, stock, plans, and real-world conditions change.

How can we trust the numbers?

Every number is calculated in code and linked to its source. Outturn shows the assumptions, trade-offs, and evidence behind each recommendation so teams can review how the decision was prepared.

What does Outturn need to get started?

A pilot can begin with the reports and exports your team already uses. Outturn learns your goals, KPI definitions, decision rules, and ownership structure before connecting more deeply with your systems.

How does the pilot work?

The four-week pilot focuses on one high-value decision area using your own data and business rules. Together, we measure how much context Outturn prepares, which decisions it surfaces, and what happens after action is taken.
