The problem

Retail teams keep reconstructing decisions the business has already faced.

The information already exists across systems, reports, conversations and people. But it is rarely available together when a commercial decision needs to be made.

Teams repeat the same work, depend on individual memory and lose the opportunity to learn from what happened next.

Outturn brings the company’s memory to the moment of decision.

01 · Fragmented context

The information exists. Just not around the decision.

The relevant context may live across sales and inventory data, planning reports, policies, previous decisions, conversations and the experience of people across the business. Teams can access each source individually. The difficult part is knowing what matters for the decision in front of them.

Commercial teams spend valuable attention reconstructing the situation before they can apply judgment.

02 · Company memory

The company has seen this before. But the knowledge is difficult to reuse.

A similar situation may have happened last season. A related choice may have been discussed in a trading meeting. A policy may already exist. Someone elsewhere in the organization may know what worked and what did not. But this memory is rarely available when a new decision needs to be made.

03 · Combined impact

Decisions make sense individually. Their combined impact may not.

Retail teams are not necessarily unaware of one another’s work. Major plans are coordinated. But commercial decisions happen across thousands of products, stores, channels and time periods. A choice that is reasonable for one team may reinforce, weaken or contradict activity elsewhere in the business.

Human coordination can cover the largest decisions. It cannot consistently evaluate every smaller, interconnected choice.

04 · Lost judgment

Operational systems record the action. The judgment is lost.

Retail systems can show that a price changed, an order was placed, inventory moved or a campaign launched. They rarely preserve the decision behind the action: the alternatives considered, the trade-off accepted, the assumptions that mattered, the expected result and the people whose experience shaped the choice.

What operational systems record

  • Decision

  • Expected outcome
  • Relevant expertise

05 · Disconnected outcomes

The outcome arrives later. Disconnected from the decision.

Commercial outcomes often become visible days, weeks or months later. By then, the original context may have disappeared. The result appears in a dashboard or report, but it is no longer clearly connected to the expectation, assumptions and alternatives present when the decision was made.

Without that connection, performance can be reviewed, but learning is difficult to reuse.

06 · More intelligence

More AI will create more recommendations. Not necessarily more learning.

Forecasting models, planning tools, copilots and AI agents will produce more signals and proposed actions. The organization will still need to know what requires a decision, which context matters, when human expertise should be involved, what was ultimately decided and whether the choice worked.

Outturn creates the memory and learning layer between commercial intelligence and human judgment.

The decision system

Every decision should make the next one better.

Outturn prepares commercial decisions using what the business knows: its data, documents, past decisions and people. It preserves the judgment applied to each choice and connects the decision with what happened next.

So when a similar situation returns, the team does not have to start again.
