The problem

Every commercial decision begins with a search.
Too few become knowledge.

Teams search systems, reports, conversations and people to understand what matters. Once the decision is made, operational systems record the action, but not the context, trade-offs, expertise or expected outcome behind it.

When a similar situation returns, the business has to search again.

Outturn brings the company’s memory to the moment of decision.

01 · Before the decision

Every important commercial decision begins with a search.

Teams search reports, dashboards, documents, emails, chats and colleagues before they can even start making the decision.

The information exists, but it is scattered across systems and people instead of being assembled around the decision.

The decision itself is often not the hardest part. Finding everything needed to make it is.

02 · After the decision

Execution is recorded. The decision is not.

Once the decision has been made, operational systems record what happened: a price changed, inventory moved, an order was placed or a campaign launched.

They rarely preserve why the decision was made, which alternatives were considered, what trade-offs were accepted or what outcome was expected.

When a similar situation returns, teams search again. The same thinking is repeated. Experience does not compound.

  • Relevant expertise

What gets squeezed out

The work that improves the business gets less attention.

When teams spend their time reconstructing context and responding to immediate decisions, less attention remains for category strategy, assortment reviews, long-tail opportunities and learning from outcomes.

At the same time, commercial decisions are made across different teams, systems and priorities. Individually they may make sense. Together they may reinforce, weaken or contradict one another without anyone seeing the combined effect.
