The problem

Every commercial decision begins with a search.
Too few become knowledge.

Teams search systems, reports, conversations and people to understand what matters. Once the decision is made, operational systems record the action, but not the context, trade-offs, expertise or expected outcome behind it.

When a similar situation returns, the business has to search again.

Outturn brings the company’s memory to the moment of decision.

01 · Before the decision

Every important commercial decision begins with a search.

Teams search reports, dashboards, documents, conversations and colleagues because the information exists, but is not assembled around the decision.

The decision itself is often not the hardest part. Finding everything needed to make it is.

02 · After the decision

Execution is recorded. The decision is not.

Operational systems record the action, but rarely preserve the context, alternatives, trade-offs, expertise or expected outcome behind it.

When the situation returns, teams search again. Experience does not compound.

What operational systems record
  • Action
  • Quantity
  • Date
  • Status
What organizations need to remember Missing from the record
  • Decision
  • Context
  • Alternatives
  • Trade-offs
  • Reasoning
  • Expected outcome
  • Relevant expertise

What gets squeezed out

The work that improves the business gets less attention.

Less attention remains for strategy, assortment reviews, long-tail opportunities and learning. Meanwhile, decisions made across teams may reinforce, weaken or contradict one another without their combined effect being visible.
